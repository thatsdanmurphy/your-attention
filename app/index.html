This site estimates the monetary value generated from your attention in real time.

Platforms report their annual revenue and user counts publicly. Divide revenue by users, divide by minutes per day, and you get a rate: what one person's attention is worth, per second. The second number multiplies that rate across the full platform.

The individual amount is small. The scale is not. Switch the platform below to see how the economics differ.

Broadcast TV at its peak ran a similar rate per second. The arithmetic of attention isn't new — the scale is, and so is what gets collected alongside it. Streaming inverts the model: you pay for the right to give them your time.

Based on FY2025 results (Meta) and FY2024 results (YouTube, Google Search) from published annual reports. TikTok figures from analyst estimates (2024 ad revenue ~$18B). All numbers are global averages.

Some moments pull everyone's attention to the same place at the same time.

The counter shows what that collective attention is worth — not to you, but to the systems capturing it. Ad revenue, broadcast rights, sponsorships, platform surges — all flowing through a single event.

The Super Bowl. Election Night. A Twitch streamer's subathon. Different shapes of attention. Different economies. Same arithmetic.

Figures from Nielsen, published earnings reports, and analyst estimates. Per-second values calculated from total event revenue divided by broadcast duration.
